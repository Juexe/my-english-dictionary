ACROSSACROSSRELATED WORDSto go across a place 穿过某地 GOon the other side of something 在某物的另一边 OPPOSITE1 ACROSS across /əˈkrɒsǁəˈkrɔːs/ [preposition/adverb] from one side of something to the other 穿过；越过 The children ran across the road. 孩子们跑过马路。 I've always wanted to sail across the Atlantic. 我一直想驾船横渡大西洋。 We gazed across the valley. 我们凝望着山谷的那一边。 The traffic was heavy so it took a long time to get across. 交通很拥挤，要花很长时间才能开过去。across to He walked across to the window. 他走到窗前。 over /ˈəʊvəʳ/ [preposition] going from one side of something to the other, especially by flying, jumping, climbing, or using a bridge 从一边到另一边，越过〔尤指飞过、跳过、爬过或过桥〕 A cat jumped over the fence. 一只猫跳过篱笆。 the road over the mountains 翻过山岭的路 one of the bridges over the Rhine 莱茵河上的一座桥 through /θruː/ [preposition/adverb] from one side or end of something to the other -- use this about going through a town, a forest, or a crowd, or looking through a hole, window etc 穿过〔小镇、森林、人群等〕；通过〔洞口、窗户等〕 I pushed my way through the crowd. 我推推挤挤穿过人群。 walking through the forest 步行穿过森林 We drove through Baltimore on our way to Washington. 我们开车去华盛顿的路上穿过巴尔的摩。 I could see her through the window. 我能通过窗户看到她。 The trip through the tunnel takes about 40 minutes. 隧道的行程大约是40分钟。 We found a gap in the fence and climbed through. 我们发现篱笆上有个缺口，就爬了过去。 trans- /trans-, trænz-/ [prefix] transatlantic/transcontinental/trans-European etc going a long distance across a large area of land or water 横跨大西洋的/横贯大陆的/横贯欧洲的等 transatlantic flights 横跨大西洋的班机 the first transcontinental railroad 首条横贯大陆的铁路 cross /krɒsǁkrɔːs/ [intransitive/transitive verb] to go from one side of something to the other, for example to cross a river or road, or to cross a field or room 横渡〔河流或道路〕；穿过〔田野或房间〕 Antonia went to cross the street to buy us some sodas. 安东尼娅穿过马路去给我们买些苏打水。 He plans to cross the Himalayas on foot. 他计划徒步翻越喜马拉雅山脉。 How are we going to cross the river? 我们打算怎么过河? Before you cross, make sure there are no other cars coming. 过马路之前要看清楚没有其他车辆开来。 cut across /ˌkʌt əˈkrɒsǁ-əˈkrɔːs/ [transitive phrasal verb] to go straight across something such as a field or road to save time, instead of going around it or by a longer way 抄近路穿过，横越〔农田、马路等〕 Farmers have begun putting up fences to prevent visitors from cutting across their land. 农民们开始建起篱笆，以阻止游客在他们的田地上抄近路穿过去。 They moved south along the edge of a field, then cut across Highway 18. 他们沿着一片田野的外围南行，然后在18号公路上横穿过去。 cut through /ˌkʌt ˈθruː/ [transitive phrasal verb] to go across an area such as a garden, a wood, or a group of buildings, instead of going around it on a path or road, because it is quicker 抄近路穿过〔花园、森林、建筑群等〕 Instead of taking the main road I cut through the churchyard and jumped over the wall at the bottom. 我没有走大路，而是抄近路穿过教堂墓地，走到尽头时跳过了围墙。 Her house wasn't far, if he cut through the woods. 他要是在树林里抄近路，她家就不是很远。 crossing /ˈkrɒsɪŋǁˈkrɔː-/ [countable noun] a journey across an area of water or group of mountains 横渡，横越 The Atlantic crossing took nearly three months. 横渡大西洋花了将近三个月。 His party made the first east-west crossing of the Sierra Nevada in 1833. 1833年，他带领的一行人首次由东向西翻过内华达山脉。
