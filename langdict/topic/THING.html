You can use thing when you mean a physical object, or something such as an event, an idea, or something that someone says 东西；物体；事情〔thing可用来指实质的物件，也可以指一件事情、一个想法或某人说的话〕 What's that thing on the kitchen table? 厨房桌子上的那东西是什么？ There were several things that I wanted to discuss. 有几件事我想讨论一下。 I cried during the whole thing. 整个过程中我一直在哭。 The first thing we need to do is call Becky. 我们要做的第一件事是给贝姬打电话。adjective thing thing We went to the aquarium, and they have this neat new thing where the tide goes in and out over a rock pool. 我们去了水族馆，他们弄了个巧妙的新玩艺，让潮水在一个岩石潭里涌进涌出。 something /ˈsʌmθɪŋ/ [pronoun] a thing - use this especially when you do not know what the thing is, or you have not decided yet what it will be 〔不知名的或尚未确定的〕某件东西，某物 I need to get something for Greg - it's his birthday tomorrow. 我得买点东西给格雷格，明天是他生日。 There's something on your shirt, on the back. 你的衬衫上有什么东西，在背上。 Something went wrong with the pumping system in the pool. 水池的抽水系统出了些问题。something something adjective Do you want to hear something really funny? 你想听点非常有趣的事吗? He wanted to get her something special, something expensive. 他想给她买点什么特别的、贵重的东西。something else another thing 其他事〔物〕 I've just remembered something else I wanted to tell you. 我刚想到还有件事要告诉你。something to object /ˈɒbdʒɪktǁˈɑːb-/ [countable noun] a separate solid thing, especially one that you can touch or hold in your hand 物品，物件〔尤指可触摸或握在手里的〕 Children should be able to point to each object as they count 别买任何工艺品，除非它们有出口许可证。2 several things of different types 不同类型的几样东西 things /θɪŋz/ [plural noun] use this when you are talking about two or more things of different types and you do not need to say what
