Could you tell me where the station is, please? 对不起，我迷路了。你能告诉我车站在哪儿吗？ Eventually the three children realized they were lost. 最后，那三个孩子意识到他们迷路了。 not know where you are /nɒt nəʊ ˌweəʳ juː ˈɑːʳ/ [verb phrase not in progressive] if you do not know where you are, you do not recognize the place that you are in 不知道自己在哪里 I really don't know where we are - can I take a look at the map. 我真的不知道我们现在在哪里了—我来看一下地图吧。 By this time she no longer knew where she was, and was beginning to panic. 这个时候，她已经不知道自己身在何处，开始惊慌了。2 to become lost 迷路，走错路 get lost /ˌget ˈlɒstǁ-ˈlɔːst/ [verb phrase] I'll give you a map so that you don't get lost. 我给你一张地图，这样你就不会走错路了。 Sorry we're so late. We got lost. 对不起，我们来得这么晚，我们走错路了。 lose your way /ˌluːz jɔːʳ ˈweɪ/ [verb phrase] if you lose your way, you go in the wrong direction or take the wrong road when you are trying to go somewhere 走错路 The climbers had lost their way in the dark. 登山者在黑暗之中走错了路。 If you lose your way, just stop and ask someone. 如果走错路，就停下来问问别人。 lose your bearings /ˌluːz jɔːʳ ˈbeərɪŋz/ [verb phrase] to become confused about which direction you should be going in, in a place that you do not know well 迷失方向 She soon lost her bearings in the dense forest. 她很快就在这片密林中迷失了方向。 I was trying to get to the A22 and lost my bearings a bit in all the country lanes. 我想去A22公路，但是这么多条乡间小路，我有点找不到方向了。3 a place where it is easy to get lost 容易迷路的地方 maze/labyrinth /meɪz, ˈlæbərɪnθ/ [countable noun] a place where it is very easy to become lost, because it has a large number of paths, passages, or narrow streets that cross each other 迷宫；迷宫一样的地方 maze/labyrinth of The old town is a labyrinth of narrow streets, with shops selling everything you could imagine. 那古镇犹如迷宫一般，狭窄的街道纵横交错，两旁的商店出售各式各样的商品。 The complex is a maze of car-ramps and driveways with signs pointing in all directions. 这座综合大楼就像迷宫一样，匝道、车道错综复杂，指路牌比比皆是，指向四面八方。
